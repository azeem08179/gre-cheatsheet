Question Type Strategies

Quantitative Comparison (QC)
  • Test special values: 0, 1, −1, fractions, large numbers
  • Answer changes with different values → choose (D) Cannot be determined
  • Simplify both columns — add/subtract same amount on both sides
  • Never multiply by a variable unless you know its sign
  • Columns algebraically equal → answer is (C)
  • Figures not necessarily drawn to scale
Multiple Choice & Problem Solving
  • Plug in numbers when variables are abstract
  • Back-solve from answer choices — start with middle option (B or C)
  • Estimate: GRE rewards smart approximation
  • Eliminate clearly wrong choices first
  • Watch for "Select ALL that apply" — check every option
  • No penalty for wrong answers — always guess if stuck

Power Tricks

🔁 Difference of Squares — Mental Math Weapon
Use (a+b)(a−b) = a²−b² for lightning-fast arithmetic.
e.g. 53 × 47 = (50+3)(50−3) = 2500 − 9 = 2491. Also: 97 × 103 = 10000 − 9 = 9991.
🧮 Vieta's Shortcut — Never Solve Quadratics You Don't Have To
GRE asks "sum of roots" or "product of roots"? Read directly from coefficients:
sum = −b/a    product = c/a. Saves 60+ seconds per question.
📏 Multiply Near 100
97 × 96 = ?   Differences from 100: 3 and 4.
Left digits: 100 − 3 − 4 = 93   Right digits: 3 × 4 = 12 → Answer: 9312
🔲 Square Numbers Ending in 5
65²: Take leading digit(s) × (itself + 1) = 6 × 7 = 42, then append 25 → 4225.
Also: 75² = 5625    85² = 7225    95² = 9025
🎯 "At Least One" Probability — Always Use Complement
P(at least one) = 1 − P(none at all).
Direct calculation is messy. Complement is always clean. e.g. P(at least one head in 5 tosses) = 1 − (½)⁵ = 1 − 1/32 = 31/32.
📐 Consecutive Integers Sum Shortcut
Sum from a to b = (# of terms) × (average of first + last).
Sum = (b−a+1) × (a+b)/2
e.g. Sum of 1 to 100 = 100 × 50.5 = 5050.

Common GRE Traps

Number & Algebra Traps
  • Variables ≠ positive integers — always test 0, negatives, fractions
  • √x² = |x|, not x — absolute value trap
  • Inequality flip — must flip when × or ÷ by negative
  • Successive discounts ≠ added — 20% + 30% ≠ 50% off
  • Percent vs percentage points — completely different concepts
  • Average ≠ median — don't confuse them
Geometry Traps
  • Co-interior angles = 180°, NOT equal
  • Inscribed angle = ½ arc, not the full arc
  • Circle equation center: (x−3)² → center is x=+3 (sign flips)
  • Height ≠ slant height in cones and pyramids
  • Diagonal of rectangle ≠ side — use √(l²+w²)
  • GRE figures NOT necessarily to scale — trust numbers, not pictures
